Citizens for Justice and Peace

Organisation Overview

Founded in the aftermath of the 2002 Gujarat carnage, CJP has grown into India's most consequential civil rights organisation — built on constitutional values, legal advocacy, and grassroots action.

Founded
April 1, 2002
Established as a collective citizen response to the communal carnage in Gujarat, with Teesta Setalvad and Javed Anand as primary founders.
Legal Registration
Society & Trust (India)
Registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860 and the Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950. Donations exempt under Section 80(G) of the Income Tax Act.
Funding Policy
100% Domestic Contributions
CJP does not accept contributions from any foreign sources. It depends entirely on contributions from Indian citizens and organisations.
Headquarters
Mumbai, Maharashtra
P.O. Box 28253, Juhu Post Office, Juhu, Mumbai – 400049. Ph: 26602288 / 26603927.
🎯
Core Mission

To uphold and defend the freedoms and constitutional rights of all Indians — especially the marginalised. CJP promotes communal harmony, builds public opinion for justice, and checks threats to Indian democracy and the Rule of Law.

⚖️
Concerned Citizens Tribunal

CJP established a citizens' tribunal headed by Justice VR Krishna Iyer (retd., Supreme Court) and two other retired judges. Their 3-volume report, "Crime Against Humanity," remains the most authoritative document on the Gujarat violence nationally and internationally.

🏛️
Best Bakery Case Victory

In April 2004, the Supreme Court of India transferred the Best Bakery case to Maharashtra — overturning the acquittal of 21 accused and ordering fresh investigation and trial. A landmark judicial milestone in post-riot justice.

📋
Zakia Jafri Case

CJP and Zakia Jafri moved the Supreme Court, seeking investigation of key evidence that the SIT had overlooked in its closure report on the Gujarat 2002 violence — demanding comprehensive accountability.

Ideology

The Four Pillars of CJP

CJP's work is anchored in four foundational areas that together form a comprehensive framework for justice, rights, and democratic accountability in India.

01
🕊️
Minority Rights

Protecting the rights of religious, ethnic, caste, gender and sexual minorities as well as persons with disabilities — ensuring no community is left without constitutional protection.

02
🗣️
Freedom of Expression

Championing free expression and the dignity of all belief systems, cultures, and languages — while firmly opposing hate speech as an abuse of democratic freedom.

03
🏛️
Criminal Justice Reform

Advocating for democratisation of investigation, prosecutorial, and judicial agencies to ensure quality, transparency, and timely justice delivery for all citizens.

04
📖
Child Rights & Education

Inculcating pluralism and constitutional values in young minds through KHOJ, while advancing Juvenile Justice Reform and child protection from sexual abuse.

Key Achievements

Impact & Milestones

From landmark Supreme Court victories to grassroots legal empowerment, CJP's record of achievement remains unmatched in post-Independence India.

01
68 Gujarat Carnage Cases

Since 2002, CJP has battled over 68 cases related to the Gujarat carnage, resulting in convictions of powerful perpetrators — a record for convictions in any religious riot in independent India's history.

02
170+ Total Convictions

CJP's legal interventions led to over 170 convictions across nine major riot incidents, with 124 perpetrators awarded life imprisonment — a historic record.

03
12 Lakh NRC Applications Supported

CJP helped over 12 lakh Indian citizens in Assam compile documents and file NRC applications, while also helping 50+ eligible detention centre inmates obtain bail.

04
Forest Rights for 7,000+ Adivasis

Trained Adivasi and forest-dwelling people to file Community Forest Rights claims across 60 villages, and filed 150+ complaints against false prosecution of Adivasi rights defenders.

05
KHOJ — 56,000 Students, 850 Schools

CJP's flagship KHOJ education programme has reached 56,000+ students in over 850 schools, with 1,300 teachers trained to inculcate pluralism and constitutional values.

06
200+ Hate Speech Petitions

Through its "Hate Hatao: Nationwide" initiative, CJP has filed over 200 petitions with relevant authorities identifying hate speech targeting minorities, Dalits, Adivasis, women, children, and LGBTQIA+ individuals.

Sonam Wangchuk
Engineer, Activist & Reformer

A mechanical engineer who became a school reformer, a sustainability innovator, and ultimately India's most visible protest voice for Ladakh's constitutional rights. Wangchuk is the real-life inspiration behind the character Phunsukh Wangdu in the Bollywood film 3 Idiots (2009).

Inventions & Organisations

Key Innovations by Sonam Wangchuk

Wangchuk's innovations range from climate adaptation technology to education reform — each deeply rooted in Ladakh's geography, people, and culture.

Signature Innovation
🧊
The Ice Stupa

An artificial glacier that stores water in winter by spraying it into freezing mountain air, where it forms cone-shaped towers of ice. As temperatures rise in spring, the ice melts gradually — providing water for irrigation during Ladakh's driest period. The technology addresses glacial retreat caused by climate change and has attracted international attention as a low-cost climate adaptation solution.

📅 October 2013 Prototype: Created a 6-metre ice stupa in Leh, freezing 150,000 litres. Water piped from upstream by gravity — no electricity or machinery. The stupa did not fully melt until 18 May 2014, even at 20°C+.
🏫
EST. 1988
SECMOL — Students' Educational & Cultural Movement of Ladakh

Founded in 1988 by a group of students who felt victimised by an alien education system. Wangchuk designed the SECMOL campus to run entirely on solar energy — using no fossil fuels for cooking, lighting, or heating. A living model of sustainable education.

🌱
EST. 1994
Operation New Hope

A 1994 collaboration between government, village communities, and civil society to reform Ladakh's government school system. Brought locally relevant curricula, community ownership, and improved learning outcomes to thousands of Ladakhi students.

🏛️
EST. RECENT
HIAL — Himalayan Institute of Alternatives, Ladakh

Promotes experiential, sustainable, and locally relevant higher education tailored to Ladakh's unique context. Gitanjali J Angmo, Wangchuk's wife, is co-founder. HIAL is envisioned as an alternative to conventional urban higher education.

📰
1993–2005
Ladags Melong

Edited Ladakh's only print magazine for over a decade — giving the region its own media voice and providing a platform to preserve Ladakhi culture, language, and local affairs for the wider public.

Protest Chronology

Timeline of Sonam Wangchuk's Protests

From sub-zero hunger strikes in Leh to a 1,000-kilometre march to Delhi — the full documented timeline of Wangchuk's protest movement for Ladakh's constitutional rights.

❄
January 2023 · Leh, Ladakh
Five-Day Fast in Sub-Zero Temperatures

Wangchuk observed a five-day hunger strike in sub-zero temperatures in Ladakh, demanding constitutional safeguards for the region — specifically Sixth Schedule status and statehood for Ladakh. This marked the formal beginning of his organised protest movement.

✓ Completed
🌿
6 March – 26 March 2024 · Leh, Ladakh
21-Day "Climate Fast"

Wangchuk launched a 21-day hunger strike describing it as a "Climate Fast" — drawing simultaneous attention to Ladakh's fragile ecosystem and reiterating demands for constitutional safeguards, after several rounds of talks with the Central Government failed to produce an agreement. He completed the fast on 26 March 2024 and announced relay strikes and a planned march to Delhi.

✓ Completed — 26 March 2024
🚶
September – October 2024 · Ladakh to Delhi
Delhi Chalo Padyatra — Nearly 1,000 km March

Wangchuk and more than 100 supporters began a nearly 1,000-kilometre march from Ladakh to Delhi. When the group reached Delhi on 1 October 2024, Delhi Police detained Wangchuk and several other protesters, citing prohibitory orders in force in the capital. Peaceful demonstrations continued after the detentions but demands remained unresolved.

Marched · Detained at Delhi Border
✊
10 September 2025 · Leh, Ladakh
35-Day Hunger Strike & Violent Crackdown

The Leh Apex Body launched a 35-day hunger strike after talks with the Centre failed. Wangchuk joined the fast. Protests escalated into violence — protesters pelted stones and burned down the BJP office in Leh. Police fired tear gas and carried out baton charges. At least 4 people were killed and about 50 were injured including 30 police personnel. Wangchuk was detained under the National Security Act (NSA).

Violent Crackdown · Detained under NSA
🔓
14 March 2026
Released After 170 Days of NSA Detention

Wangchuk was released from detention after 170 days under the National Security Act. Following his release, Home Minister Amit Shah visited Ladakh on 30 April and 1 May 2026, and a fresh round of talks with the Central Government was held on 22 May 2026 — though the principal demands remained unresolved.

✓ Released · Talks Resumed
🏛
28 June 2026 — Ongoing · Jantar Mantar, New Delhi
Indefinite Hunger Strike — Jantar Mantar, Delhi

Wangchuk began an indefinite hunger strike at Jantar Mantar in New Delhi — this time in solidarity with a youth-led movement demanding accountability over exam irregularities (NEET) and the resignation of Union Education Minister Dharmendra Pradhan. On Day 21, police took Wangchuk to Safdarjung Hospital as his health critically deteriorated. The Delhi High Court directed authorities to monitor his health daily.

🔴 Ongoing · Hospitalised Day 21

Key Figures

Central Personalities

The individuals whose vision, sacrifice, and leadership have defined these two extraordinary movements in modern Indian history.

👩
CJP · Secretary & Co-founder
Teesta Setalvad

Indian civil rights activist and journalist who co-founded CJP with Javed Anand in 2002. Has spent decades fighting in court for the victims of the Gujarat riots. Awarded Padma Shri in 2007 for contributions to Public Affairs.

🌸 Padma Shri, 2007
👨
CJP · Co-founder
Javed Anand

Journalist turned minority rights activist and Teesta Setalvad's husband. Co-founded CJP in 2002. Awarded the Knight of the Order of Merit by the French Government in 2005 for his civil rights work in India.

🇫🇷 Knight of the Order of Merit, 2005
🏔
Activist & Innovator · Ladakh
Sonam Wangchuk

Engineer, education reformer, environmentalist, and protest leader. Founder of SECMOL (1988) and inventor of the Ice Stupa. Ramon Magsaysay laureate (2018). Currently (July 2026) on an indefinite hunger strike at Jantar Mantar, Delhi.

🏆 Ramon Magsaysay, 2018
